Introduction

In the spirit of curiosity and experimentation, I recently embarked on the journey of creating a DIY Egg Incubator. Driven by the desire to witness the magic of life, I utilized a thermostat as a temperature sensor and controller, a 100-watt heat bulb, a fan for air circulation, and a water tray for humidity maintenance. Although the chosen hen eggs didn't hatch as expected, the thrill of seeing the machine function flawlessly was a rewarding experience in itself.

Key Components

Thermostat as Temperature Sensor and Controller :
A crucial element for maintaining the optimal temperature inside the incubator.

100-Watt Heat Bulb :
Provided the necessary warmth for the eggs to incubate.

Fan for Air Circulation :
Ensured uniform temperature distribution and promoted a stable environment for incubation.

Water Tray :
Implemented to maintain humidity levels essential for successful egg incubation.



Experimentation and Challenges :
 Choosing hen eggs that were more than 14 days old turned out to be a learning experience. While the eggs didn't hatch, the project's primary objective was to validate the functionality of the incubator. This trial-and-error process offered insights into the intricacies of egg incubation and highlighted the importance of selecting eggs at the right developmental stage.

Incubator Operation :
 The thermostat played a crucial role in the incubator's operation, continuously monitoring and adjusting the temperature to create an ideal environment for egg incubation. The heat bulb provided a consistent heat source, while the fan ensured proper air circulation for temperature uniformity. The water tray contributed to maintaining the necessary humidity levels within the incubator.

Joy in Success, Lessons in Failure :
 Despite the eggs not hatching, the success of the incubator in maintaining the desired conditions was a triumph in itself. The project illuminated the challenges and variables involved in egg incubation, reinforcing the value of precision and attention to detail in such endeavors.
