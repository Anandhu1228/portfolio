Introduction

In the pursuit of beating the heat and staying cool on a budget, I embarked on a DIY project to create a Portable Air Cooler. This compact yet powerful cooling solution incorporates a 12-volt motor, operated by a 12-volt adapter, and harnesses the cooling magic of evaporation. Unfortunately, detailed images are unavailable due to a damaged device, but the performance and ingenuity of the cooler are not compromised. Let's dive into the details of this efficient and portable cooling device.

Key Components

12-Volt Motor :
Powers the air cooler, ensuring a continuous flow of refreshing air.

Evaporation Cooling Principle :
Utilizes the cooling effect of water evaporation to reduce ambient temperature.

Mesh Panels :
Positioned on the sides and rear, facilitating the flow of air through water-saturated material for effective cooling.

Submersible Pump :
Circulates water to the mesh panels, enhancing the cooling process.

Compact Design :
Portable and space-efficient, making it suitable for various environments.

Water Level Indicator :
Provides a visual cue to monitor the water level inside the cooler.



Experimentation and Challenges :
 The air cooler operates on the principle that evaporation of water absorbs heat from the air, resulting in a cooling effect. A submersible pump ensures a constant flow of water to the mesh panels, where air passing through picks up moisture, creating a refreshing breeze.

Portability and Performance :
 Despite its compact size, this DIY air cooler does not compromise on performance. Its portability makes it a versatile cooling solution, easily adaptable to different spaces and situations. Perfect for cooling small rooms, personal spaces, or outdoor environments.

Water Level Indicator :
 To enhance user convenience, a water level indicator is incorporated into the design. This feature provides a quick and easy way to check the water level inside the cooler, ensuring optimal functionality and preventing the motor from operating without sufficient water.
