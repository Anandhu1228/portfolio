Guitarist

Self learned from youtube

I've always harbored a dream of playing an electric guitar on an open stage, fueled by the electrifying scenes of heroes wielding guitars in movies. This fascination gradually drew me towards English bands like Megadeth, Guns N' Roses, Metallica, Green Day, Led Zeppelin, Judas Priest, Linkin Park, and many others. I developed a profound respect for iconic guitarists such as Slash, Eddie Van Halen, Ozzy Osbourne, Jimmy Page, and numerous others.

However, my passion took a decisive turn when I heard Green Day's "Boulevard of Broken Dreams" from the album "American Idiot." Inspired, I made the bold decision to transition from merely listening to playing the electric guitar. Despite my parents' lack of interest, I diverted funds initially earmarked for a laptop to purchase a guitar. With no amplifier and no knowledge of playing, I turned to YouTube as my teacher. Through countless mistakes and errors, I eventually learned to play and even recorded videos without an amplifier.

A supportive friend, believing in my potential, contributed half the amount for an amplifier. Combined with my savings, I managed to acquire one. Despite the constraints of academic commitments and other passions, I allocate precious time for practice. Post-graduation, I envision dedicating myself to intensive practice, aiming to evolve into a professional guitarist by the time I reach 28 years old.
